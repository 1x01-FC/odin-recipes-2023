Avocado Cookies

Description

Link

Soft and generous cookies!! Long live the lawyer!

Ingredients

  • 1 C. (15 ml) white chia seeds + 2 tbsp. soup (30 ml) of water
  • 1 1/2 cups (375 ml) gluten-free flour or regular flour
  • 3 tbsp. (45 ml) cornstarch or tapioca starch
  • 1 C. 1/2 tsp (5 mL) baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp. 1/2 tsp (1.25 mL) salt
  • 1/3 cup (80 mL) brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup (125 ml) coconut oil, at room temperature
  • 1/2 ripe avocado, mashed (1/4 cup)
  • 2 tbsp. (10 ml) vanilla
  • 1 cup (250 mL) semi-sweet, dairy-free chocolate chips or chunks
  • 1/3 cup (80 mL) pecans, to garnish

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, soak the chia seeds in the water. To book.
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, baking soda and salt. To book.
  3. In another bowl, using an electric mixer, beat the brown sugar, sugar, egg, coconut oil, mashed avocado and vanilla for 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the swollen chia seeds.
  5. Using a spatula, add the dry ingredients and the sprinkles and mix well. Let the dough cool for at least 1 hour in the refrigerator. This step is very important, because if the dough is not cold enough your cookies will flatten.
  6. Preheat the oven to 375°F and place the rack in the middle. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper.
  7. Form dough balls of about 2 tbsp. or use a small ice cream scoop and drop onto baking sheets, leaving 2” space between cookies. Slightly flatten tops of cookies and top with pecans.
  8. Bake 10 minutes. Do not overcook! The cookies won't look fully cooked in the center, that's what we want! Let the cookies cool for 3 minutes on the baking sheets.
